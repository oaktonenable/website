Flagship Project

The IsoWrite

A revolutionary assistive writing device engineered to counteract the involuntary tremors of Parkinson's disease — restoring one of life's most personal forms of expression.

For the over 10 million people worldwide living with Parkinson's disease, something as simple as signing a birthday card or keeping a journal can be a painful reminder of what the disease has taken. The IsoWrite is our answer — and we're building it from scratch.

The Problem

Parkinson's Robs the Simple

Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurological disorder affecting the dopamine-producing neurons in the substantia nigra. Among its most visible and debilitating symptoms is resting tremor — involuntary rhythmic shaking of the hands and limbs that persists even when muscles are at rest, making precision tasks nearly impossible.

These tremors make fine motor control profoundly difficult. Writing, once second nature, becomes a daily struggle. Medication helps in early stages, but tremors often persist or worsen over time, and pharmaceutical options carry serious long-term side effects that limit their utility for many patients.

Writing is not just functional — it is deeply personal. Losing the ability to write a letter, sign your own name, or journal your thoughts is a loss of identity and autonomy. It is a quiet indignity that millions face, and one that existing assistive tools have not yet fully solved.

Our Solution

A Pen That Thinks in Real Time

The IsoWrite is a smart assistive writing device that uses onboard inertial sensors to continuously monitor hand movement across multiple axes. By applying signal processing algorithms, the device distinguishes between the involuntary frequency signatures of Parkinson's tremor and the intentional, lower-frequency movements of natural writing.

Once tremor motion is isolated and characterized, a precision counteraction mechanism works to neutralize the unintended displacement at the pen tip — applying corrective force in real time so the user's writing surface remains smooth, stable, and controlled regardless of the tremor's severity.

IsoWrite doesn't try to eliminate Parkinson's disease. It works around it — giving patients the freedom to write naturally again, without the frustration, the fatigue, or the loss of dignity that comes with untreated tremor.

Technical Design

How It Works

Step 01 — Sensing
Tremor Detection
High-precision IMUs embedded in the pen body continuously monitor acceleration and rotation across all axes, capturing the unique tremor signature of the user's hand movement at hundreds of samples per second.
Step 02 — Processing
Signal Analysis
An onboard microcontroller runs filtering algorithms in real time to separate the involuntary tremor oscillations (typically 3–8 Hz for Parkinson's) from intentional writing movements, which occur at lower frequencies.
Step 03 — Actuation
Countermovement
The isolated tremor signal drives a precision mechanical or electromechanical stabilization system that applies an equal and opposite corrective displacement to the pen tip — neutralizing tremor motion at the point of contact.
Step 04 — Output
Stable Writing
The net result at the pen tip is smooth, controlled, and consistent — producing clear, legible handwriting regardless of tremor severity. The patient writes naturally. The pen handles the rest, silently and continuously.
